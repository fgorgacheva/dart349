Artist's Statement

As a person, I am someone who is quite emotional and particularly nostalgic. Memories make me happy, sad, fill me with sadness for what is gone and with joy for what has been.

As an artist, creating projects that touch upon those distant memories throws me on trips down memory lane! This is why I decided to recreate a little story from my childhood that my mother had taught me from a young age. It is still very relevant to me to this day; it comes up to my mind quite often as I continue to meet people who have not had the fortune of learning the moral of the story at an earlier age.

Stories drive me. I’m a very visual person, so whenever I read stories, I have entire movies play out in my head. Either way, I see more and more that I love stories. From a very young age, I became obsessed with video games because they not only allowed me to learn a new story like in books, but it allowed me to be the main character, and on top of that, it was also like a movie! 3 in 1! I love games, books, tv shows, anecdotes, prompts, and all the things that make my mind run and imagine scenarios!

In summary, this project was meant to reflect and share my passion for stories, my love for my memories and my bittersweet attachment to nostalgia. It was also a lot of fun to figure out how to make something I’ve never made before!
